CERN (European Laboratory for Particle Physics) is an international laboratory where the W and Z bosons were discovered in 1983, and then in 2012 the Higgs boson was discovered. CERN is the birthplace of the World-Wide Web. The Large Hadron Collider (see below) will continue searching for other new fundamental particles and forces.

Overall view of the LHC experiments

The Large Hadron Collider inhabits the same tunnel as the LEP.

The LHC accelerates two beams of protons to 7000 GeV in two adjoining 27-kilometer magnets, where they circulate 11,000 times a second. The particles, going opposite directions, are steered into head-on collisions in the experiment halls. In other experiments, it can accelerate entire large nuclei up to 600 TeV.

The accelerator magnets are kept at 1.8 degrees above absolute zero during operation so that they will be superconducting.
